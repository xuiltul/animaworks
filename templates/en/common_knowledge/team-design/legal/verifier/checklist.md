# Legal Verifier — quality checklist

Checklist for Legal Verifier to protect verification quality with or without machine.

---

## A. Optimism bias (Devil’s Advocate gate)

- [ ] Re-evaluated every “acceptable” / “no further negotiation” item
- [ ] For each “acceptable” item, considered worst case if the counterparty maximized that clause
- [ ] No hand-waving like “the counterparty has no reason to refuse”
- [ ] Risk not lowered for non-legal reasons (e.g. “might harm the relationship”)
- [ ] Downgrades vs last round have enough legal support
- [ ] Effort standards (best efforts / reasonable efforts) not read to the softer side without basis

---

## B. Coverage (missed issues)

- [ ] Every prior finding appears in this report
- [ ] No Critical from last round with no mention this round (silent drop)
- [ ] Clause count in contract vs clauses discussed in report
- [ ] Non-amended clauses unchanged vs prior version
- [ ] Definition changes reflected in risk view
- [ ] Schedules/exhibits reviewed

---

## C. Legal accuracy

- [ ] Cited statute numbers are correct
- [ ] Cited cases (court, date, docket) are correct
- [ ] “Industry standard” claims have support (or Researcher was asked if missing)
- [ ] Terms used correctly (e.g. duty of care, warranty) — no misuse
- [ ] Analysis matches governing law (domestic vs foreign)

---

## D. Negotiation strategy

- [ ] Split between “must fix before sign” vs “OK after sign” is sensible
- [ ] Post-sign bargaining power considered
- [ ] Counterparty position analysis included
- [ ] Priority order makes sense (risk × negotiability)
- [ ] Actions are concrete and executable

---

## E. Meta-review quality

### Machine verification output

- [ ] Removed false positives
- [ ] Closed false negatives
- [ ] Critical/Warning/Info severities are right
- [ ] Findings are factual, not speculative

### Feedback quality

- [ ] Critical items include rationale and suggested fix
- [ ] Optimism-bias items include worst case
- [ ] Tone is constructive (issue + improvement)
- [ ] carry-forward gaps cite the prior report
